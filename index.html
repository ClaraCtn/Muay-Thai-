Origins & History

Muay Thai, known as the "Art of Eight Limbs," originated in the ancient kingdom of Siam (now Thailand). Developed as a form of close-combat that used the entire body as a weapon, it was heavily utilized by Siamese soldiers on the battlefield.

Over centuries, it evolved from a deadly military tactic into a highly respected ring sport. Today, it stands as Thailand's national sport and a globally practiced martial art, celebrated for its raw power, discipline, and profound cultural roots.

Wai Kru Ram Muay

The Wai Kru Ram Muay is a traditional ritual dance performed by fighters before a bout. "Wai" is a traditional Thai greeting of respect, and "Kru" means teacher. "Ram Muay" translates to the boxing dance.

This sacred ritual serves multiple purposes: it pays homage to the fighter's teachers, parents, and ancestors; it acts as a physical warm-up; and it symbolically seals the ring to protect the fighters from bad spirits. It is a profound display of the spiritual side of Muay Thai.
